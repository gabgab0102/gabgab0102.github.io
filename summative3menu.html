MECHADO

GAB'S CUISINE

The name "mechado" comes from the Spanish word "mechar," meaning "to stuff" or "to lard," referring to the technique of inserting strips of fatback into the beef, which adds moisture and flavor.

The main ingredients include beef, soy sauce, calamansi, black pepper, onions, and sometimes potatoes and carrots.

Mechado stands out from other Filipino tomato-based stews like kaldereta, afritada, and menudo with its tangy, savory flavor profile, and the use of larding.

MENUDO

GAB'S CUISINE

The word "menudo" comes from Spanish, meaning "small bits".

The sauce is known for its sweet and savory flavor, with the tomatoes providing a bright, acidic counterpoint to the richness of the pork and liver.

The dish typically features cubed pork, sliced pork liver, potatoes, carrots, and sometimes bell peppers, all simmered in a tomato-based sauce.

CALDERETA

GAB'S CUISINE

The name "caldereta" comes from the Spanish word "caldera," meaning "cauldron," reflecting the traditional method of cooking the stew in a large pot.

Caldereta is a beloved comfort food in the Philippines, enjoyed during family gatherings, fiestas, and special celebrations. Caldereta is often served hot with white rice, making it a hearty and satisfying meal.

AFRITADA

GAB'S CUISINE

The name "afritada" comes from the Spanish word "fritada," meaning "fried," reflecting the initial step of sautéing the meat and aromatics before stewing.

The meat is first sautéed, then simmered in the tomato sauce along with the vegetables until tender.

Afritada is typically served hot over white rice.
